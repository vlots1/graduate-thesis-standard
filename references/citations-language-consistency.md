# Citations, Language, and Consistency

## Citation and reference rules

- **Mandatory:** Follow GB/T 7714. Use either numeric citation or author–year citation consistently across the whole thesis. `R p.5`.
- **Mandatory:** Every cited work appears in the reference list; no uncited work appears there. Put read-but-uncited supplementary works in an appendix named `参考书目`. `R p.5`.
- **Mandatory:** Numeric systems list records in citation-index order (and may group by type). Author–year systems sort by author and year, with Chinese records first. `R p.5`.
- **Mandatory:** References begin on a new page after the body. `R p.5`.
- **Recommended:** Validate type-specific fields against the selected GB/T 7714 version, not APA/MLA. The rule shows examples for serials, books, proceedings, theses, web resources, patents, and standards. `R p.5`; `W paras 411-465`.
- **Manual:** Completeness and factual truth of author, DOI, URL, dates, and page ranges need bibliographic verification. Never auto-correct a reference merely because it looks unusual.

## Language and terminology

- **Mandatory:** Write normally in Chinese using `通用规范汉字表`; a foreign-language teaching program may use another language but must include Chinese title and abstract. `R p.3`.
- **Mandatory:** Keep terms, symbols, codes, and designations uniform. Explain a professional term/abbreviation at first appearance. For foreign terms/abbreviations, give the full original term in parentheses after the first translated occurrence. Use legal Chinese measurement units. `R p.3`.
- **Mandatory:** Figures/photos must be complete and clear. `R p.3`.
- **Mandatory content standard:** Main text must be truthful, objective, logically layered, concise, readable, accurately expressed, and academically ethical. It should not fabricate, plagiarize, ghostwrite, or falsify. `R pp.2-5`.
- **Not a school format rule:** Preferred person, voice, stylistic elegance, general statistics notation, Chinese-English spacing, full/half-width use beyond the specifically stated keyword separator, and units/significant figures beyond legal units. Apply a relevant discipline/style guide and label as recommendation.

## Cross-document audit checks

Check the following, marking semantic judgments `manual` when automation cannot establish equivalence:

1. Contents entries, page numbers, and hierarchy match headings in the main file.
2. Figure/table lists match captions; sequence, caption placement, and all in-text cross-references match their objects.
3. Equation labels and in-text references match, including appendix restart.
4. Every in-text citation has one reference record and every record is cited.
5. Chinese/English abstracts agree on purpose, method, sample size, main results, conclusion, and keyword correspondence.
6. Terms, abbreviations, variable names, group labels, chapter numbers, appendix labels, page-number regime, and heading levels remain stable.
7. Sample sizes and statistical values are internally identical across abstract, methods, results, tables, figures, discussion, and conclusion. Treat a mismatch as a blocker candidate; do not repair it automatically.
